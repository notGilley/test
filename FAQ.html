Frequently Asked Questions



Welcome to the FAQ section! Here, you'll find answers to some common questions about building a computer. If you have any other questions, feel free to reach out!



1. What is the process of building a computer?

Building a computer involves selecting compatible components such as the CPU, motherboard, RAM, storage, power supply, and case. These components are then assembled, connected, and configured to work together. After assembly, the system is tested to ensure it functions correctly, and an operating system is installed.

2. Why is there a build fee?

The build fee covers the time, expertise, and effort required to assemble and configure the computer. It ensures that the components are installed correctly, the system is tested for stability, and any potential issues are resolved. This fee compensates for the professional service provided, saving you time and ensuring a reliable build.

3. Can I build a computer myself?

Yes, you can build a computer yourself if you have the necessary knowledge and tools. However, it requires research, patience, and attention to detail. If you're unsure or want to avoid potential mistakes, hiring a professional is a good option.

4. How long does it take to build a computer?

The time required to build a computer depends on the complexity of the build and the experience of the builder. On average, it can take anywhere from 2 to 4 hours, once all the parts have arrived, including assembly, testing, and software installation.

5. What if I have issues after the build?

If you encounter any issues after the build, please reach out for support. Depending on the nature of the problem, we can provide troubleshooting assistance or arrange for further service.
